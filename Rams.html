    Ground Penetrating Radar

    Ground Penetrating Radar (GPR) is a non-invasive geophysical method used to investigate subsurface structures and materials. It employs radar pulses to image the underground, providing valuable data for applications such as utility mapping, archaeological investigations, and geological assessments. GPR is essential for ensuring safety and accuracy in various surveying projects by revealing hidden features without the need for excavation.

    Falling Weight Deflectometer

    The Falling Weight Deflectometer (FWD) is an essential tool for assessing the structural integrity of pavements and road surfaces. It measures the deflection response of the pavement when a known weight is dropped, providing data on the load-bearing capacity and overall condition. FWD results are crucial for making informed decisions regarding maintenance, rehabilitation, and design of road infrastructure.

    Retro Reflectometer

    Retro Reflectometers are an essential tool for assessing the visibility and safety of road markings, which are critical for guiding drivers and pedestrians. We can measure horizontal and vertical reflectivity as per NHAI TOR and provide valuable data for determining the condition of road markings and signs.

    Mobile Bridge Inspection Unit

    The MBIU uses a combination of cameras, sensors, and other devices to collect data on the structural integrity of the bridge. It can access hard-to-reach areas, such as underneath the bridge, to provide a comprehensive assessment of the condition of the structure. The MBIU can identify areas that require maintenance or repair, as well as potential safety hazards.
