    Foundation

    Foundations are the essential base of any structure, providing stability and ensuring the safe transfer of loads from the building to the ground. They are critical in preventing settlement, structural damage, and uneven load distribution. Foundations are typically categorized into shallow and deep types, depending on the soil conditions and the size of the structure. Shallow foundations, such as strip or spread footings, are suitable for lighter buildings, while deep foundations, like pile or raft foundations, are used for larger structures or where soil conditions require additional support. Properly designed and constructed foundations are vital for the long-term safety and integrity of any construction project.
